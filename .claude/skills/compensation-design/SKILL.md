# Compensation & Benefits Design

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you need to:
- Build a salary band structure for all roles
- Design variable pay and incentive schemes
- Create a benefits package competitive in your market
- Conduct market benchmarking for specific roles

---

## Core Principle

PAY FOR VALUE DELIVERED, NOT JUST TIME SPENT — A CLEAR AND FAIR COMP STRUCTURE IS YOUR STRONGEST RETENTION TOOL.

---

## Phase 1: Brief

| Input | What to Ask | Default |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Market** | Which labor market? (Bangkok, regional, global?) | Bangkok |
| **Industry** | Competing with tech, FMCG, consulting? | As per company |
| **Positioning** | Pay at, above, or below market? | At market (P50) |
| **Structure** | Fixed only, or fixed + variable? | Fixed + performance bonus |
| **Budget** | Total comp budget as % of revenue? | 30–40% for early stage |

---

## Phase 2: Salary Band Structure

### Grade System Example
| Grade | Role Examples | Salary Range (THB/month) |
|-------|--------------|--------------------------|
| G1 | Coordinator, Junior Specialist | 18,000–28,000 |
| G2 | Senior Specialist, Team Lead | 28,000–45,000 |
| G3 | Manager, Senior Manager | 45,000–75,000 |
| G4 | Director, VP | 75,000–130,000 |
| G5 | C-Suite | 130,000+ |

*Adjust ranges based on your market benchmarking data.*

### Band Rules
- Each band has a min (P25), midpoint (P50), and max (P75)
- New hires typically start at P25–P50 based on experience
- Promotions move to new band minimum + increase
- Annual merit increases: 3–8% based on performance rating

---

## Phase 3: Variable Pay Design

### Performance Bonus Structure
| Rating | Bonus as % of Annual Base |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Exceptional (5) | 20–25% |
| Strong (4) | 15% |
| Meets Expectations (3) | 8% |
| Needs Improvement (2) | 0% |

### Sales Incentive Plan (if applicable)
- Base salary: 60–70% of total comp
- Commission: % of revenue or margin above quota
- Accelerators: Higher % for >110% quota achievement

---

## Phase 4: Benefits Package

### Mandatory (Thailand)
- Social Security contribution
- Provident Fund (minimum 3% employer match)
- Annual leave: 6–10 days minimum
- Public holidays: 13 days

### Market Competitive (Recommended)
- Health insurance: IPD + OPD, family extension option
- Performance bonus: 1–3 months
- Training budget: ฿5,000–฿15,000/year per person
- Flexible working or WFH policy

### Premium (Talent Attraction)
- ESOP / profit sharing
- Wellness allowance
- Learning & development stipend
- Additional leave (birthday, volunteering)

---

## Anti-Patterns
- Paying below market then wondering why you can't retain
- Secret salaries creating internal inequity and distrust
- Variable pay with targets no one understands
- Benefits no one uses (ping-pong tables over health insurance)
